My Three Priorities

What I'll fight for in Dáil Éireann

1

Public Transport

Galway is gridlocked. We need GLUAS, more buses, and better trains to free up the city.

In Galway, public transport funding has stalled under this Government, and if elected I will fight to restore it.

2

Cost of Living

The Energy Crisis is a Fossil Fuel Crisis. I will push for direct supports for the most vulnerable now.

We need more renewables and innovative solutions like plug in solar to get off fossil fuels so that this energy crisis is the last.

3

Community

Our communities deserve spaces to gather, celebrate, and play.

I will work for more sports facilities, community centers, and pedestrian areas in the city, continuing my work on Galway City Council.

About Niall Murphy

I have lived and worked in Galway for the past thirty years as a software engineer for multinationals and medical device companies. I previously represented Galway City West as a city councillor for four years.

I live in Salthill with my wife Una. Our three children are now in their early twenties, and I see first-hand the life challenges faced by a new generation.

Outside of politics I am a runner and swimmer, and I have organised many cycling and running events in the city.

4 years as Galway City Councillor representing City West
Past Board Member of Leisureland and University of Galway
Community organizer for cycling, running events, and youth sports
Environmental campaigner for clean water in Galway Bay
10 years as hurling coach for underage teams
